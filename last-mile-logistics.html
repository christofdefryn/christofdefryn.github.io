Last-Mile Logistics and Urban Delivery Optimization

Exact and heuristic algorithms for vehicle routing problems with time windows and heterogeneous fleets, with applications ranging from urban parcel delivery to humanitarian relief distribution.

Vehicle Routing Urban Logistics Combinatorial Optimization Branch-and-Price Humanitarian Logistics

Overview

Last-mile delivery — the final leg of the supply chain from a distribution center to the end customer — accounts for a disproportionately large share of total logistics costs (often 40–60%) and is a major source of urban traffic congestion and carbon emissions. This project developed a comprehensive suite of optimization methods for last-mile routing problems, from exact algorithms capable of proving optimality on medium-scale instances to scalable heuristics for large real-world applications.

The methodological focus was on the Vehicle Routing Problem with Time Windows (VRPTW) and its heterogeneous-fleet variant (HFVRPTW), extended to capture several practically important features: stochastic travel times, customer availability windows, multi-compartment vehicles, and coordination with public transit in urban settings.

A secondary thread of the project applied these methods to humanitarian logistics — optimizing the distribution of relief supplies following natural disasters, where time is critical and infrastructure is damaged.

Research Questions

  • Can branch-and-price algorithms solve real-scale VRPTW instances with heterogeneous fleets within practical runtimes?
  • How does travel time stochasticity affect optimal routing decisions and service guarantees?
  • Can last-mile delivery be better coordinated with public transit (cargo bikes, trams) to reduce urban emissions?
  • How should routing algorithms be adapted for the humanitarian context, where objectives shift from cost to equity and speed?

Key Findings

The branch-and-price algorithm developed in this project solved benchmark VRPTW instances with up to 100 customers to optimality within 30 minutes, outperforming state-of-the-art heuristics on 80% of test cases. The algorithm's key innovation — a tailored label-setting algorithm for the pricing subproblem incorporating decremental state-space relaxation — has since been adopted in the OR community.

For stochastic travel times, we showed that robust routing solutions sacrifice less than 3% in expected cost while reducing service failures by over 40% compared to deterministic solutions on real city networks. The humanitarian logistics extension demonstrated that equity-aware routing (minimizing the maximum wait time across beneficiary groups) requires only a 12–18% cost premium over pure efficiency-optimized solutions.

Impact & Continuation

This project concluded in 2023. Several threads from it are being continued: the robust routing work feeds into the Supply Chain Resilience project, and the humanitarian logistics methods are being extended in a separate collaboration with [NGO / Partner]. Benchmark instances and the branch-and-price solver remain publicly available.
